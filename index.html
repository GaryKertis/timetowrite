about

Time to Write is a workshop-based writing program for twelve to eighteen year olds. We offer a supportive, dynamic, and fun atmosphere for kids to work on their writing and critical feedback skills.

Each workshop will allow at least one student to read their work aloud and receive constructive feedback from their peers and teachers. A portion of each class session will be dedicated to writing; this may take the form of a generative writing prompt or focused revising/rewriting exercises. Feedback sharing will be structured to ensure that students feel supported and respected and that suggestions for improvement are focused, specific, and constructive. Students will closely read and interpret examples of short stories, essays, and excerpts of longer works. We will use these examples to discover effective elements of writing, and writing prompts will provide opportunities for students to try on these craft and style techniques in their own writing. This is an immersion program for kids who would like more time to work on their creative writing pursuits or work specifically on an essay for school or school admission. We also offer custom-tailored (bespoke?) private tutoring for students who are pursuing admission to middle school, high school, or college. Each workshop is team taught by Polly and Sarah who have spent most of their adult lives teaching and writing. We are excited to offer kids a Time to Write!

teachers

Polly Duff Kertis

...

Since receiving her MFA in Fiction Writing at Sarah Lawrence College in 2012, Polly Duff Kertis has been teaching creative writing to students ranging in age from ten to eighty. She has taught group workshops and worked as a private consultant at Sarah Lawrence College, Sackett Street Writers’ Workshop, and Gotham Writers’ Workshop.

Her writing has appeared in Tin House, The Literary Review, The Collagist, The Brooklyn Rail, Literary Mama, and other journals. She has completed a collection of short stories and is working on a novel. She is the author of two chapbooks of translation, OLD GUS EATS (Publishing Genius, 2012) and MIRROR POEMS (O’Clock Press, 2012).

She founded the Brooklyn reading series Writers Reading to Writers Listening to Writers Reading to Writers. She co-founded the biennial Moby-Dick Marathon NYC.

She lives in Brooklyn with her husband and two kids.

Sarah Moore

...

Sarah has an MA from NYU in writing and performance and she has been working with kids for over twenty years. She graduated from Brown University with a BA in theatre and went on to teach poetry, solo performance, and creative writing as a teaching artist for various companies in dozens of public schools. She is working on a memoir about her work as a doula in NYC and has two children who constantly amaze her with their thoughtful and insightful writing.
